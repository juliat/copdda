Optimizing Medical Treatment: Summary

Advantages of Optimizing Medical Treatment Disadvantages of Optimizing Medical Treatment
You will probably be able to be able to go without oxygen. Even if you are eligible to get a transplant, you might be unlucky and may never get lungs that are a match.
You will probably be able to get around more. You could walk your dog or go on a walk with a friend, be able go back to work or volunteering, or even play sports. You have to take a lot of drugs (dozens per day), and some of them may make you sick.
You could be more independent. You could be able to drive again, to cook for yourself, and do house or yard work on your own. The transplant process is stressful for you and even more stressful for your caregiver.
You can spend time with family and friends and be more able to keep up (especially with grandkids.) Complications from the transplant could make you have to stay in the hospital for months or undergo painful medical treatments. Some complications may lead to death.
You’ll be able to travel (particularly on airplanes.) After the transplant you have to visit the transplant doctors regularly and get tests and you may have to live close to the hospital.
Lots of little things change: there’ll be foods you can’t eat, places you shouldn’t go, and things can’t do.
Transplant is expensive.
