Optimizing Medical Treatment

Optimizing medical treatment means working closely with doctors to get the best type of treatment for you. Doctors can adjust or switch your medicines, help find exercise that works for you, and work on making your day-to-day life better.

Lung Transplant Surgery

Lung Transplantation takes out your lungs and put in new lungs from an organ donor. After the surgery you will need to take many different medications to keep your body from attacking the lungs. When you leave the hospital, you will need to measure your vital signs (like your blood pressure) and your spirometry (lung function tests) almost every day for the rest of your life. You will also need to get your blood work checked and see your doctor often. Sometimes your doctor will want to see you once a week.
